Blending Mode List

PhotoWizard uses standard blending modes found in Photoshop and other image editing software.

 

These modes are essential for creating various effects and achieving precise control over the blending of images.

 

The following table contains a brief explanation of each mode:

 

Mode Description
Normal No blending. The top layer covers the bottom layer.
Multiply Darkens the image by multiplying the bottom layer with the top layer. The result is always darker.
Screen Lightens the image by inverting, multiplying, and inverting again. The result is always lighter.
Overlay Combines Multiply and Screen modes. It darkens dark areas and lightens light areas.
Darken Compares each pixel of the two layers and keeps the darkest one.
Lighten Compares each pixel of the two layers and keeps the lightest one.
Dodge (Colour Dodge) Brightens the bottom layer based on the brightness of the top layer.
Burn (Colour Burn) Darkens the bottom layer based on the brightness of the top layer.
Soft Light Softens the image by darkening or lightening it, depending on the top layer.
Hard Light Adds harsh contrast by combining Multiply and Screen. It results in intense effects.
Difference Subtracts the top layer from the bottom layer, or vice versa, to get the absolute difference.
Exclusion Similar to Difference but with lower contrast.
Saturation Applies the saturation of the top layer while keeping the hue and brightness of the bottom layer.
Hue Applies the hue (colour) of the top layer while keeping the brightness and saturation of the bottom layer.
Colour Applies the hue and saturation of the top layer while keeping the brightness of the bottom layer.
Luminosity Applies the brightness (luminosity) of the top layer while keeping the hue and saturation of the bottom layer.
Darken Only Similar to Darken but more intense.
Lighten Only Similar to Lighten but more intense.
