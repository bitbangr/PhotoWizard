What is Masking?

Image masking lets you selectively edit parts of your photos to enhance details or isolate subjects.

Use PhotoWizard masking tools such as the Magic Wand for colour-based selection and the Brush for manual adjustments.

Masking provides precise control over which parts of your image are edited.

Core Masking Applications

  • Selective Application of PhotoWizard Filters or Effects:

    • Use masks to apply PhotoWizard effects or filters to specific parts of your image.

      For example, you can preserve the colour of a rose while converting the background to black and white.

  • Creating Photos with Transparency:

    • Use masks to create images with transparent areas, which is beneficial for web design, graphic design, and video production.

PhotoWizard Masking Tools and Functions

  • Eraser: Removes unwanted mask areas.

  • Magic Wand: Masks all the similarly coloured pixels connected at the touch point.

  • Colour Range: Masks all similarly coloured pixels across the image.

  • Brush: Allows for manual mask drawing with a brush tool.

  • Lasso: Enables precise area selection with a lasso tool.

Geometric and Gradient Masks

  • Rectangle and Ellipse: Draw masks using geometric shapes.

  • Linear, Bi-linear, and Radial Gradients: Create gradient masks.

Additional Masking Controls

  • Invert Mask: Toggle masking between inside and outside a selected area.

  • Clear Mask: Remove all masks from the image.

  • Blur Mask: Soften mask edges for a smoother appearance.

  • Undo and Redo: Correct mistakes or revert changes in masking.
